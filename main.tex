\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

The current landscape in video-language modeling is paradoxical. Video-LLMs can generate fluent descriptions and answer many semantic questions from RGB video \cite{zhang2023video, damonl2024videollama2, li2024llavanext}. However, when questions require stable 3D structure over time, such as tracking objects through occlusion, comparing motion under viewpoint changes, or maintaining correct temporal causality, performance degrades sharply. This gap is now quantified by spatiotemporal benchmarks, which reveal substantial deficits in temporal coherence, viewpoint-aware motion understanding, and physical plausibility \cite{zhou2025vlm4d, zhu20254dbench, wang2025spatial4dbench}.

These trends point to a central conclusion: the bottleneck is not a missing ``module'', but the backbone representation itself. This proposal targets the missing piece: a backbone that learns a unified 4D latent world representation from the most available modality, timestamped 2D video. We introduce 4D-MLLM as a 4D latent world model transformer designed around (1) changing the token definition from 2D patches to 4D entities, (2) modifying attention to be biased by spatiotemporal proximity, and (3) shifting the representation target from ``video features'' to a unified 4D latent world representation. These changes aim to bridge semantic video understanding and structured 4D (3D+time) reasoning using ubiquitous 2D video inputs.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\subsection{Sequence-based Reasoning}
Foundational Video-LLMs like Video-LLaMA 2 \cite{damonl2024videollama2} and LLaVA-NeXT-Video \cite{li2024llavanext} have established high semantic benchmarks by processing video as 2D patch sequences. However, these models often suffer from ``temporal flickering'' and lack physical grounding, as evidenced by deficits in benchmarks like VLM4D \cite{zhou2025vlm4d} and Spatial4D-Bench \cite{wang2025spatial4dbench}. Recent 2026 updates, such as the introduction of Spatio-Temporal Rotary Positional Embeddings (ST-RoPE) in Qwen2.5-VL and Gemini 2.0, attempt to bridge this ``signal-to-structure'' gap. Despite these advances, the newer DynPhys-Bench \cite{dynphysbench2026} indicates that sequence-based models still struggle with non-linear motion and occlusions, suggesting that a transition to continuous 4D manifold representations is necessary.

\subsection{Native 4D Perception}
Emerging works have moved beyond simple 4D point cloud alignment toward Neural Field-based architectures. Early native 4D systems like 4DPC²hat \cite{4dpc2hat2026} and B4DL \cite{b4dl2025} ingest dynamic point cloud or LiDAR sequences to align 4D geometry with language. This has evolved into more sophisticated frameworks like Uni4D-LLM \cite{zhou2025uni4dllm}, which unifies 4D understanding and generation. The latest 2026 models, such as 4DLangVGGT \cite{wu20264dlangvggt}, utilize 4D Gaussian Splatting (4D-GS) as a mid-level representation. Unlike prior methods requiring explicit 3D supervision, current Gaussian Splatting-based LLM (GS-LLM) architectures leverage Differentiable Rendering Layers to backpropagate language-based grounding losses directly into latent 4D scene graphs \cite{wang2025vggt}, bridging raw pixels and structured geometry.

\subsection{Latent World Models}
A parallel direction involves injecting geometric priors or learning intrinsic world models. Geometry-augmented VLMs like DSR Suite \cite{dsrsuite2025} and PSG-4D-LLM \cite{psg4dllm2025} utilize reconstruction-derived tokens or 4D panoptic scene graphs to enhance spatial reasoning. This trend has culminated in the 2026 emergence of Latent World Models (LWM) such as NVIDIA Cosmos \cite{nvidia2026cosmos} and OpenAI Sora-v2 \cite{openai2026sora}. These models demonstrate that massive video pre-training on billions of frames can lead to ``emergent 4D geometry,'' where consistent 3D views can be synthesized from 2D inputs. 4D-MLLM builds on this by formalizing these emergent properties into a backbone paradigm that ensures representations are both visually plausible and geometrically grounded.

\subsection{Embodied 4D Action}
In robotics and egocentric vision, 4D awareness is critical for translating perception into action. Models like 4D-VLA \cite{4dvla2025} and STLLM \cite{stllm2025} integrate spatiotemporal grounding for action planning and environment reasoning, often fusing global scene point clouds with local video frames. More recent 2026 developments like SteerVLA \cite{steervla2026} further integrate 4D perception for real-time trajectory planning using 4D-Affordance Maps. While these models are primarily task-oriented, 4D-MLLM aims to provide the foundational ``world engine''---a backbone that maintains a time-continuous and geometry-consistent 4D representation suitable for both passive understanding and active robotic manipulation.

\section{Proposed Method}
\label{sec:methodology}

We propose a backbone that transforms the fundamental unit of computation from a ``2D patch'' to a ``4D token''. Concretely, for each token, we maintain a tuple $T_i = (f_i, x_i, \tau_i)$, where $f_i$ is the visual feature (appearance), $x_i$ is a learned latent 3D coordinate that organizes tokens into a geometry-consistent space, and $\tau_i$ is the absolute timestamp associated with the observation. The key point is that $x_i$ is not ground-truth geometry and does not rely on explicit reconstruction; it is learned as part of the representation target so that the backbone naturally supports questions of ``what is where, when'' and can maintain object permanence across views and time.

To learn $x_i$ from timestamped 2D video, we induce geometry through self-supervised spatiotemporal consistency rather than SLAM-style pipelines. We train the model to align tokens that correspond to the same physical entity across time and viewpoint changes, enforcing stability for static regions ($x_{i,t} \approx x_{j,t+1}$) while allowing structured evolution for dynamic regions. Time is modeled as a continuous variable using learnable Fourier features to handle variable frame rates and precise durations. Importantly, the model learns absolute time understanding by encoding real-world timestamps directly in the representation, which enables consistent reasoning over long videos, irregular sampling, and multi-camera synchronization. We represent dynamics with a time-conditioned predictor that relates changes in $x$ to elapsed time, encouraging temporal scaling consistency.

On top of these 4D tokens, we modify the attention mechanism to reflect spatiotemporal structure: attention is biased so that tokens that are close in latent 3D space and close in time interact more strongly than distant ones, supporting locality and causal propagation in the learned world model. We train the backbone with Masked 4D Modeling by masking a spatial region (in latent space) or a temporal interval and requiring reconstruction from the remaining context, which encourages both occlusion reasoning and trajectory completion. We evaluate 4D-MLLM as a backbone rather than a task-specific head, using benchmarks that expose 4D reasoning gaps including VLM4D \cite{zhou2025vlm4d}, 4D-Bench \cite{zhu20254dbench}, and Spatial4D-Bench \cite{wang2025spatial4dbench}, with comparisons to strong video-language baselines \cite{damonl2024videollama2, li2024llavanext} and ablations over token definition, attention bias, and representation objectives.

\section{Expected Outcomes}
\label{sec:outcomes}

4D-MLLM is expected to deliver a backbone that moves beyond sequence modeling and produces a stable, evolving 4D latent world representation that is useful across downstream tasks. In practice, this should translate into stronger 3D-aware question answering (e.g., resolving relative depth and spatial relations under viewpoint changes), more reliable metrology-style reasoning (e.g., inferring speed and trajectory from changes in latent position over absolute time), and improved occlusion reasoning through object permanence, where entities remain represented even when temporarily invisible in the raw video stream.
