\begin{abstract}
The landscape of Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) has shifted rapidly from static 3D understanding to \textbf{Native 4D} reasoning (2025-2026). Models like 4DPC²hat and B4DL now explicitly process dynamic point clouds and LiDAR sequences, achieving strong performance in semantic captioning and QA. However, a critical \textbf{"Metrology Gap"} remains: while these systems can describe \textit{what} is happening (e.g., "a car is turning"), they lack the physical grounding to quantify \textit{how} it happens (e.g., precise velocity, acceleration, or force). This proposal introduces a framework for \textbf{Physically-Grounded Native 4D Understanding}, integrating state-of-the-art dynamic geometry encoders with physics-informed training objectives. By enforcing kinematic consistency within the MLLM's latent space, we aim to enable precise metrological reasoning alongside semantic understanding.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

The evolution of video understanding has reached a pivotal inflection point. Work from mid-2024 to early 2026 demonstrates a move beyond "static 3D + language" towards explicitly \textbf{4D (3D + Time)} architectures. Native 4D MLLMs like 4DPC²hat \cite{4dpc2hat2026} and B4DL \cite{b4dl2025} have shown that processing dynamic geometry directly—rather than relying solely on 2D video frames—significantly enhances spatiotemporal reasoning. Similarly, frameworks like DSR Suite \cite{dsrsuite2025} prove that injecting explicit "geometry tokens" can augment standard Video-VLMs.

Despite these advances, current 4D models predominantly focus on semantic tasks: captioning dynamic scenes, generating scene graphs, or answering qualitative questions. They falter when tasked with \textbf{Metrology}: the precise quantification of physical dynamics. Questions like "What is the instantaneous velocity of the vehicle?" or "Did the collision force exceed the safety threshold?" require more than pattern matching; they demand an internalization of physical laws. This research asks: \textit{Can we adapt Native 4D architectures to not only understand semantic change but also accurately quantify the physics of motion?}

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

This proposal builds upon the "Native 4D" wave of 2025-2026.
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Native 4D MLLMs:} 4DPC²hat \cite{4dpc2hat2026} sets the standard for dynamic object understanding using Point-BERT and Bidirectional Mamba backbones. B4DL \cite{b4dl2025} extends this to outdoor scenes with 4D LiDAR encoders.
    \item \textbf{Hybrid & Geometry-Augmented Frameworks:} STLLM \cite{stllm2025} combines global static 3D contexts with local egocentric video for embodied reasoning. DSR Suite \cite{dsrsuite2025} introduces a "Geometry Selection Module" to inject 4D priors into video models.
    \item \textbf{Structured 4D Reasoning:} PSG4D-LLM \cite{psg4dllm2025} and 4D LangSplat \cite{4dlangsplat2025} link LLMs to 4D scene graphs and Gaussian Splatting fields, respectively, facilitating open-vocabulary querying of dynamic scenes.
    \item \textbf{Robotics:} 4D-VLA \cite{4dvla2025} leverages 4D pretraining for robust robotic manipulation, highlighting the value of spatiotemporal grounding for action.
\end{itemize}
Our work unifies these directions, specifically targeting the under-explored intersection of Native 4D processing and precision metrology.

\section{Methodology: Enabling Physically-Grounded 4D Understanding}
\label{sec:methodology}

Our approach fuses explicit 4D structural learning with physics-informed supervision.

\textbf{Constructing a Hybrid Synthetic-Real Metrology Benchmark.} 
Data scarcity is a primary bottleneck for 4D learning \cite{4dpc2hat2026, b4dl2025}. We will construct "Metrology-Bench" using a hybrid pipeline. We will leverage physics simulators (CARLA, Blender) to generate synthetic video/LiDAR data with \textit{perfect} ground-truth annotations for velocity, acceleration, and forces—attributes impossible to label accurately in real data. This will be augmented with real-world sequences (e.g., from Ego4D or nuScenes) annotated via sparse photogrammetry, following the transfer learning protocols of B4DL and PSG4D-LLM.

\textbf{Architecture: Adapting Native 4D Backbones.} 
We will adopt a **Native 4D** architecture rather than a 2D-centric one. Drawing on 4DPC²hat \cite{4dpc2hat2026}, we will utilize a **Point-BERT + Bidirectional Mamba** encoder to capture the temporal evolution of 3D geometry efficiently. To handle the high dimensionality of 4D data, we will incorporate a **Geometry Selection Module** (inspired by DSR Suite \cite{dsrsuite2025}) to dynamically select only the most relevant spatiotemporal tokens for the LLM, ensuring computational feasibility without sacrificing precision.

\textbf{Training with Kinematic Consistency Losses.} 
Standard next-token prediction is insufficient for learning physics. We will introduce a multi-task training regime. Alongside text generation, the model will feature a **Coordinate Regression Head** (similar to PSG4D-LLM's output modules) to predict future object states. Crucially, we will apply a **Physics Consistency Loss** that penalizes predictions violating Newtonian mechanics (e.g., discrete jumps in velocity implying infinite acceleration). This forces the model's latent representation to respect the continuity of physical motion.

\section{Evaluation and Validation}
\label{sec:evaluation}

We will validate our framework against state-of-the-art baselines, including **GPT-4V** (2D baseline), **4DPC²hat** (Native 4D semantic baseline), and **DSR Suite** (Geometry-augmented baseline).

\textbf{Success Criteria.} A \textbf{good result} would demonstrate that the Physics-Informed Native 4D model achieves statistically significant improvements in speed (MAE) and distance estimation compared to semantic-only 4D models. It should successfully generalize to novel viewpoints and "counter-intuitive" physical scenarios where visual heuristics fail. A \textbf{bad result} would suggest that the model merely memorizes simulation artifacts or fails to transfer physical reasoning to real-world noisy data, indicating a need for more robust domain adaptation techniques.
